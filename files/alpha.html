Alpha by Gia Howard

Alpha is a more Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math based program (STEM). This program enables your problem solving and critical thinking part of your brain through 50 different STEM courses. For reference here are a few courses that are included: Aquaponics, App Development, Engineering, Robotics, Web Design, and all STEM-related Advanced Placement courses. Along with these courses comes extracurriculars activities that will help expand on your knowledge of STEM, like GEMS (Girls in Engineering, Math, and Science), Math team, Robotics team, and Science Olympiad. a-Stem Honors Program is a program in which you can only be invited into. In ordered to be considered for this program you would have to take many strict courses in Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math. The alpha students will have to conduct a self directed research project including a Scientific paper and experiment. The research will be done at universities with the help of professors. The research will then be presented in the annual Lane Tech Science fair. As in their website a-STEM’s mission is to “ provide and support integrated curricular offerings and approaches to optimize scientific inquiry and scientific literacy learning, employ problem-solving skills through experimentation, link science and technology to the real world, provide independent research opportunities, and build partnerships with universities, businesses, and the community.” Alpha students are in Honors meaning they have to maintain an A or B. Incoming Freshmens can apply based on honors status, math and science grades, and achievement scores.
