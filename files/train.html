Train

Trains are transportation devices approximately 7.2 meters long ranging from 10-35 carriages. There are two types of trains. Transportations/ civilian use, and resource transportation. In total there are 8 civilian trains that you can take. Fun fact Chicago is one of the few cities in the world that has rail service to two major airports. Both trains are a part of a large train system called the Chicago L system The blue line is more popular than the brown line. Due to the fact that it connects directly to O'hare Airport. The blue line starts west of Chicago in the suburbs, goes straight into Chicago, then goes through the loop, and finally turns around to head toward O’hare. The blue line goes straight through the 360 interstate highway, also called Eisenhower. The brown line starts from Kimball to downtown. If you take the brown line. It will drop you off decently far from Lane Tech. That is why most students then transfer from the train to the bus. The same applies for the blue line. Although there isn’t a train that stops directly by Lane, there are many trains you can take to buses that stop in front of the school. For example, the blue line can be taken to the Western stop. When you arrive at the western stop there will most likely be a bus stop nearby that takes you to school. Other trains you can take are the red line and the brown line. The train you should take is the one that is closest to your home.
